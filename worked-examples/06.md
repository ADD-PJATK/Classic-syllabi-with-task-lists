# 2. Basic Concepts

**Clustering:** partition (or soften) points into groups so that points in the same group are **similar**.

**k-means:**

1. Pick **k** initial centroids.  
2. Assign each point to the nearest centroid.  
3. Recompute centroids as the mean of assigned points.  
4. Repeat until stable.

**Curse of dimensionality:** distances become less informative in very high dimensions; normalization and dimensionality reduction often help.

**BFR (high level):** clusters summaries (count, sum, sum of squares per dimension) in **sub-batches** for huge data that cannot be held in memory—extends the spirit of one-pass clustering.

# 3. Preparing numeric data

### Step 1

Load a CSV with **only numeric** columns (or encode categoricals separately).

### Step 2

Apply **normalization** (e.g. z-score or min–max) so that no single feature dominates distance.

### Step 3

Choose **k** (often by domain knowledge or elbow/SSE curve).
