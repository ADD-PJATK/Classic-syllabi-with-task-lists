# Session 2 – Association rule mining 2 (advanced, transactional data)

# Task 1 – Concepts

Answer in your own words:

1. What are **closed itemsets** and **maximal itemsets**? How do they differ from “all frequent itemsets”?
2. Why might we use **different minimum support** for different items (e.g. in multi-level association rules)?
3. Give **one advantage** and **one limitation** of applying association rule mining to very large transactional datasets.

---

# Task 2 – Larger transactional dataset

Obtain or create a transactional dataset with **at least 50 transactions** and **at least 5 different items**.

1. Convert or describe the dataset in a format suitable for association mining (e.g. binary matrix or transaction list).
2. Run **FP-Growth** or **Apriori** with two different **minimum support** values (e.g. 0.15 and 0.25). Use a fixed minimum confidence (e.g. 0.5).
3. Report for each run: number of frequent itemsets, number of association rules.
4. Export the **top 10 rules by lift** for one of the runs.

---

# Task 3 – Business interpretation

From your results in Task 2:

1. Choose **three rules** and for each give: rule, support, confidence, lift.
2. Interpret each rule in plain language (what behaviour or pattern does it describe?).
3. Suggest **one concrete business action** for each rule (e.g. product placement, bundle offer).

---

# Task 4 – Comparison with Session 1

Briefly compare:

1. What was different when moving from a small (Session 1) to a larger dataset?
2. How did changing minimum support affect the number and type of rules?
