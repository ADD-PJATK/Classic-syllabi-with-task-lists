# Session 7 – Clustering continued: text and web content

*Aligned with syllabus: selected applications (text mining and web content mining); lab: theoretical and practical tasks on clustering.*

---

# Task 1 – Conceptual questions

Answer in your own words:

1. Why is the **vector space model** useful for clustering documents?
2. What is **TF-IDF**? Why do we down-weight terms that appear in many documents?
3. What is **cosine similarity** between two document vectors and why is it often preferred to Euclidean distance for sparse text?
4. Name **one** problem that makes clustering web content harder than clustering small curated corpora.

---

# Task 2 – Build a tiny document corpus

Create **≥8** short “documents” (each **1–3** sentences), e.g. fake product reviews or news blurbs, in **English** or **Polish**.

1. List the documents (IDs D1…Dn).
2. Build a **document–term matrix** (counts or binary). You may use **≤30** distinct terms after simple lowercasing and removing stopwords by hand.
3. Apply **TF-IDF** (library or manual formula) and show the **shape** of the matrix (rows × cols) and **one** example row for document D1.

---

# Task 3 – Clustering documents

1. Cluster your documents into **k = 2** or **k = 3** groups using **k-means** on TF-IDF vectors (or hierarchical clustering if you prefer—state which).
2. For each cluster, list **document IDs** and **2–3** terms with highest average TF-IDF in that cluster (or interpret centroids in plain language).
3. In **3–5 sentences**, discuss whether the clusters match intuitive topics.

---

# Task 4 – Optional extension

If your tool allows, visualize documents in **2D** (e.g. TruncatedSVD on TF-IDF) and color by cluster.
